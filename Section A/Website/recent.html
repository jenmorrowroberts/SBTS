Among Others

A school librarian friend recommended this one to me and I wanted to love it. It's the story of a teenage girl who is obsessed with science fiction and fantasy and uses these novels as an escape from some pretty tragic events in her life. Mixed in with this is the idea that, for her, magic is real, as are fairies, though the reader often questions whether this is just another escape mechanism. As I said, I wanted to love this novel, as it was, I did enjoy it, it just didn't live up to its potential, I felt, but there are some great characters and lots of allusions to great scifi literature.

The Maze Runner

After hearing so many of you talk about this book, I thought I'd give it a go. As stories go, it's entertaining, if a bit predictable in parts. The writing leaves something to be desired, but it's a fun Saturday afternoon read and the first of a series. I've been loaned the second book, so maybe he'll win me over yet.

The Art of Racing the Rain

This is next up on my Nook. I know little about it other than it's narrated by a dog--how could I pass that up? It was the Fairfax Reads choice a few years ago and has good reviews. Hopefully I'll be able to pass on my own glowing reviews soon.
