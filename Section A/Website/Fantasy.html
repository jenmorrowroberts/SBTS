Traditional Fantasy

When you think of "fantasy," you probably think of magic and mystical creatures. J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis were some of the forerunnners of modern "traditional" fantasy. Currently, George R. R. Martin's Game of Thrones series is extremely popular and a fun (if violent) read. Eragon and its sequels is another series following traditional fantasy storylines you might enjoy.

Y.A. Fantasy

As with nearly every genre, a subgenre that markets directly to people your age exists and fantasy is no exception. Harry Potter is, of coures, hugely popular, as are other series like The Hunger Games, The Lightening Thief, City of Bones, and yes, even Twilight. There are literally hundreds of fantasy stories out there to sift through aimed at teenagers. Try popular reading sites like GoodReads to find ones you might like. I enjoyed Libba Bray's series linked above (sorry, boys, you probably won't enjoy that one) and just started The Maze Runner series which seems promising.

Fantasy with a sense of humor

I always enjoy a bit of wit in my reading and even in fantasy there are a few authors that turn the genre on its head. Neil Gaimen is always creative; he writes everything from children's books (seen Coraline?) to adult novels. American Gods is like Percy Jackson for grownups and when he teams up with Terry Pratchet they have a wicked sense of humor.
