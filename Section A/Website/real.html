Man vs Man

Realistic Fiction is a pretty broad category, so I'm going to talk about it in terms of conflict types. Some great works of realistic fiction set up conflicts between people, whether physical conflict such as in times of war (try Fallen Angels) or in more subtle psychological conflicts such as crime thrillers of Agatha Christie or Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. Bel Canto is appears at first to be a conflict between terrorists and their hostages but takes some unexpected twists.

Man vs Society

While many science fiction stories tackle this conflict, realistic fiction also takes on the conflict between protagonists and the society in which they find themselves. The Great Gatsby, one of my favorites, addresses this issue, as does A Confederacy of Dunces, though Toole does so in a much more biting and hilarious fashion with an extremely unlikeable, ridiculous narrator you for some reason can't help but liking.

Man vs Self

While there are of course other external conflicts in these novels, Speak, Extremely Loud and Incredibly Close, and Looking for Alaska are all incredibly internally driven books about the search for meaning amidst the seemingly uncaring world. All of them also feature young protagonists, yet are extremely diverse and all great reads.
