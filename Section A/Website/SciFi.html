Sarcastic SciFi

Some of my favorite science fiction writers are those who take a tongue-in-cheek approach to looking at the foibles of the future. Kurt Vonnegut and Douglas Adams are two such writers. Their books all offer wit and humor rather than a serious and straightforward critique of society's failings. If you're looking for more subtle stabs at what may befall humanity, try one of these authors.

Y.A. SciFi

Some of the most interesting current science fiction (especially in the dystopian realm) is coming from the young adult literature section. M.T. Anderson and Scott Westerfield are both excellent writers who offer stinging social critique and tell a darn good story. For some traditional science fiction that appeals to a young audience, browse the young adult section.

Classic SciFi

Many of the now "overused" tropes of science fiction got their start in the novels of the masters like Arthur C. Clarke, Robert Heinlein, and Phillip K. Dick. If you like science fiction, or are interested in some of the founding ideas of science fiction--like the rules for human-robot interaction--pick up a novel by one of these authors.
