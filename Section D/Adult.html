Audience:

Teachers looking for ways to maximize in-class time for practice of skills and remediation, while minimizing lecture time. The session will be offered as part of the professional development series during collaboration time.

 

Rationale:

While interactive lessons are always the goal, there are times when lectures are useful and even necessary. However, spending class time lecturing and having students then try to apply the material learned in the lectures when they are at home is not the best use of time. Students can’t ask questions that arise, which is especially disadvantageous to those students whose parents aren’t able to help them with homework.

By placing video lectures on Blackboard for students to watch for homework, class time is freed for what would be traditionally assigned as homework, when the teacher can serve as guide.

Materials needed for lesson:

Computer lab with projector and/or Vision software, and microphones

Handout on Photo Story 3

Handout: The Flipped Classroom

 

Desired learning outcomes

Know several ways to incorporate a “flipped” classroom model

Understand how to use video lectures as part of their curriculum

Be able to create videos in Photo Story 3 from previously created PowerPoint presentations

Pre-assessment data will be collected from entry ticket survey of awareness of concept, PowerPoint fluency and Photo Story experience

Formative assessment will be collected by observing class participants interaction with software and questions asked during session

Summative assessment will be collected from an exit ticket survey on understanding of software and how the model might fit in the classroom

 

Agenda

Part one: Introduction to Flipped Classroom (10 minutes)

Note: Before session, ask teachers to log in to computers and choose a PowerPoint slide they have created to work with; email sample PowerPoint to those who need it.

Explain to teachers the research and rationale behind a “flipped” classroom, open discussion on possible ways the model can be incorporated into different subject matter:

  • Math: Explain new unit and demonstrate practice problems on video, have students practice the concept in class
  • Social Studies: students watch lecture on time period at home, hold Socratic Seminar in class
  • Science: Explain background and procedures for upcoming lab on video so there’s more time for the lab in class
  • English: Watch grammar explanation video and take notes at home, practice identify parts of speech in class

Resource for research on “flipped” classroom:

"Flipping Your Classroom - 13 Must Read Resources | Poudre School District."Flipping Your Classroom - 13 Must Read Resources | Poudre School District. Poudre School District, 2010. Web. 21 Apr. 2013.

                Strategies used: Whole group discussion, handouts to follow along visually

Part two: Modeling Software (20 minutes)

Show class short sample video of writing instruction by use of projector. Sign in to Blackboard and show linked video for student access. Then, show original PowerPoint presentation.

Switch to vision (if available) and demonstrate how to save PowerPoint files as .jpg to work with Photo Story 3.

Open Photo Story 3 and model step-by-step how to import photos, record voice, manipulate animation, and export as a video. Stop to ask for questions during demonstration

Strategies used: Modeling

Part three: Practicing Photo Story 3 (20 minutes)

Review the steps involved in using Photo Story 3, referring to handout. Then release Vision so that participants have access to computers. Ask participants to follow the steps to upload their slides into a new Photo Story project.  Participants should also practice recording voice-over and manipulating animation for at least one slide before the end of the session. Move through the lab, helping participants with questions as they arise. At end of session, guide teachers through saving Photo Story projects to finish at a later date.

Strategies used: hands-on learning, guided practice
